Pedagogy

My Teaching Philosophy

I believe that all students are infinitely unique and infinitely interesting. They each have something special to offer, and I see it as my role to help cultivate whatever this may be. I emphasise the relational aspect of teaching, taking the time to get to know the whole student; academically, socially, and emotionally, to ensure every learner feels safe, respected, and connected to their peers and the broader community. I aim to create a classroom culture where curiosity, collaboration, and empathy are valued alongside academic achievement. I also exhibit high expectations and a strong work ethic in my role, modelling these traits to inspire a growth mindset in my students.

Core Values

Relationships:
I believe that trust and connection are the foundation of meaningful learning. Strong relationships create a safe environment where students feel supported to take risks.
Growth Mindset:
I encourage students to see mistakes as stepping stones toward improvement, helping them build resilience and confidence in their own learning journey.
Inclusion:
Every student deserves to feel valued and represented. I adapt my teaching to meet diverse needs so that all learners can participate fully.
Curiosity:
I aim to spark a love of discovery, encouraging students to ask questions, explore new ideas, and take ownership of their learning.

Behavioural Management

My approach to behaviour management is grounded in Positive Behaviour Support (PBS), using the three-tiered framework to provide universal strategies for all students, targeted supports for those who need additional guidance, and intensive interventions when required. I believe behaviour management is not just about discipline, but about equity and inclusion, ensuring every student has the opportunity to feel safe, respected, and able to learn. Through a social justice lens, I aim to understand the diverse backgrounds and challenges students may face, and to respond with fairness, empathy, and culturally responsive practices that build a positive, supportive classroom community.
